TASK DEFINITION

You will receive a user message containing:
- Product category and subcategory
- Materials with their weights in kg
- step_material_mapping: a dictionary mapping each material to its
  ordered list of processing steps
- Target warehouse location (EU or US)
- A seed number for variety

For each material in the product, you must:

1. Assign a realistic geographic location (City, Country) and WGS84
   coordinates (latitude, longitude) to every processing step in that
   material's chain. Choose locations where that type of processing
   actually occurs in the global textile industry.

2. For each pair of consecutive processing steps, determine the
   transport leg between them:
   - The transport modes used, as an ordered array reflecting the
     multi-modal journey (e.g., ["road", "sea", "road"] for a typical
     intercontinental shipment)
   - The total distance in km for the leg, accounting for actual
     transport routes (road distances, shipping lanes, not straight
     lines)
   - A reasoning narrative describing each segment of the journey,
     including intermediate waypoints such as ports or rail terminals

3. Converge all material chains at the assembly step. All materials
   must arrive at the same assembly location.

4. After assembly, route the finished product to the target warehouse
   as the final leg.

The allowed transport modes are: road, rail, sea, air, inland_waterway.

CRITICAL RULES (failure to follow these causes automatic rejection):

1. EVERY MATERIAL MUST HAVE TRANSPORT LEGS:
   You MUST generate at least one transport leg for EVERY material
   listed in the MATERIALS section. If a product has 3 materials, all 3
   must appear in your output legs. If a product has 4 materials, all 4
   must appear. Do NOT skip minor or secondary materials. Even a material
   at 10% weight needs its own processing chain with transport legs.
   The validator checks that every material name from the input appears
   in at least one leg's "material" field.

2. EXACT MATERIAL NAMES:
   The "material" field in every transport leg MUST be copied EXACTLY
   from the MATERIALS list in the user message. Do NOT shorten, rename,
   or paraphrase material names. For example:
   - Input "fibre, polyester" -> write "fibre, polyester"
     (NOT "polyester", NOT "polyester fibre")
   - Input "cellulose fibre" -> write "cellulose fibre"
     (NOT "cellulose", NOT "rayon")
   - Input "fibre, flax" -> write "fibre, flax"
     (NOT "flax", NOT "linen")
   The validator performs an exact string match.

3. ALL CHAINS MUST END AT THE SAME DESTINATION:
   After the assembly step, the final warehouse leg must be identical
   for all material chains. All materials must converge at the assembly
   location and the finished product must be shipped to a single
   warehouse.

4. NO ZERO-DISTANCE LEGS:
   Every leg must have distance_km >= 1.0. If two steps happen at the
   same facility, use a minimum distance of 1.0 km for the internal
   transfer.

Output ONLY a JSON array of transport leg objects. No explanation text
before or after the JSON.
