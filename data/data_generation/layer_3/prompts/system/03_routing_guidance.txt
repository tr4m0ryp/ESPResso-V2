TRANSPORT ROUTING CONTEXT

This section provides contextual knowledge for reasoning about
transport modes. These are typical patterns, not rigid rules. Use your
judgment based on the specific route geography.

Regional infrastructure context:
- South and Southeast Asia: dense road networks connect manufacturing
  clusters; major ports at Singapore, Colombo, Chittagong, Laem Chabang
- China: extensive rail network (China Railway Express to Europe);
  major ports along the eastern seaboard
- Europe: well-developed rail freight corridors; short-sea shipping in
  the Mediterranean and North Sea; dense motorway network
- North America: long-haul trucking dominant; intermodal rail for
  coast-to-coast; major West Coast ports for Asian imports

Common freight route patterns:
- Asia to Europe: container ship via Suez Canal (transit 25-30 days)
- Asia to US West Coast: transpacific container ship (transit 14-20 days)
- Asia to US East Coast: transpacific then Panama Canal, or via Suez
- Intra-Asia: short-sea shipping or road, depending on distance
- Europe internal: road or rail, often combined

Multi-modal journey structure:
- First mile: road transport from factory/processing facility to the
  nearest port, rail terminal, or airport
- Trunk haul: sea, rail, or air for the main intercontinental or
  long-distance segment
- Last mile: road transport from port/terminal to destination facility

Distance and mode relationships (general guidance):
- Under 500 km: typically road only
- 500 to 2000 km: road, rail, or short-sea shipping
- Over 2000 km: includes a sea or air trunk segment, with road first
  and last mile
- Air freight: reserved for high-value, time-sensitive, or lightweight
  shipments; rare for bulk textile transport
