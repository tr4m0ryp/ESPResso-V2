MATERIAL CONVERGENCE LOGIC

Products with multiple materials have independent processing chains
that must converge at an assembly location.

Single convergence (approximately 80% of products):
All materials arrive at one CMT (Cut, Make, Trim) factory for final
assembly. This is standard for basic garments such as T-shirts,
trousers, dresses, and shirts. The assembly step is the first step in
step_material_mapping that lists multiple materials (e.g., "garment
assembly", "stitching", "sewing"). All material chains must route to
the same city for this step.

Progressive convergence (approximately 20% of products):
Some materials converge at an intermediate sub-assembly point before
final assembly. This applies to:
- Footwear: upper components and sole components may be sub-assembled
  separately before final bonding
- Technical outerwear: shell fabric and insulation may be quilted or
  laminated at a sub-assembly step before final garment assembly
- Tailored garments: body panels and lining may be prepared separately

To identify the assembly step: look for the step in step_material_mapping
where multiple materials first appear together. Common assembly step
names include "garment assembly", "stitching", "sewing", "bonding",
"lasting", and "final assembly".

After the assembly step, all materials travel as a single unit. Post-
assembly legs use the product name (not individual material names) or
the first material listed, and represent the combined product moving
to the warehouse.
