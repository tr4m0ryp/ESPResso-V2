LOCATION VARIETY GUIDANCE

Vary your location choices across records. Do not default to the same
city for every product with similar materials.

Use the seed number provided in the user message to influence your
choices. Different seeds should lead to different location selections
for similar products. For example, cotton sourcing could come from
India, Egypt, Uzbekistan, the United States, or Brazil depending on
the seed.

Consider secondary manufacturing regions, not only the largest hubs.
Vietnam, Cambodia, Ethiopia, and Peru are growing textile producers
alongside the dominant centers of China, India, and Bangladesh.

Assign different sourcing regions for different materials within the
same product. Cotton may originate from India while polyester comes
from Taiwan or South Korea. Wool may come from Australia or New
Zealand while synthetic lining comes from China.
